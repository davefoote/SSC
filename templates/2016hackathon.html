About the event

South Side Civic a CAPP-student led group is partnering with UChicago’s Institute of Politics (IOP), Department of Computer Science, and the Harris School for the first time to host a two-day Civic Hackathon!

Our Civic ScopeAthon's main objective is to focus on a civic-minded or community-based problem, form cross-functional teams to scope out the problem, break the problem down into its parts and develop a plan to address the problem. The solutions may or may not be entirely tech-oriented, or only parts of the solution may have a tech component.

While traditional hackathons are about writing code, our scopeathon is more about understanding the purpose and use of technology in helping solve problems. Before anyone writes a line of code, it’s crucial to have a correct and complete understanding of what the full-scale technology solution will need to accomplish. This understanding needs to bridge developers and organization representatives, and the scope-athon is designed to strengthen this cross-discipline awareness.

What is Our Goal?

The event will bring together leaders of civic organizations, students, and community members to learn about civic hacking, civic problems, and how to work in teams to form a solution. Participants will come from various backgrounds, including, but not limited to, computer science, political science and public policy, economics, geography, visual art, civic innovation and leadership, etc.

  • Learn and exercise problem-scoping skills
  • Meet civic leaders in active organizations
  • Work on real problems
  • Become a part of a civic-minded tech community

FAQ

What is a Civic Hackathon?

The main objective of our ScopeAthonis to take a civic-minded or community-based problem and to form committed groups to scope out the problem, breaking it down into its parts and developing a plan to address the problem. The solutions may or may not be entirely tech-oriented, or only parts of the solution may have a tech component.

At our Civic ScopeAthon you will gain problem-scoping skills, learn about other civic-tech organizations, network with community leaders, and join a team and enjoy the thrill and excitement of a 2-day hackathon.

Where is it?

Chicago Innovation Exchange, 1452 E 53rd St, Chicago, IL 60615 2nd floor

When is it?

Saturday, October 8th.

Who should attend?

Our scopeAthon is open to the entire University of Chicago community as well as interested folks working anywhere from the public/non-profit sector to the private sector.

Participants will come from various backgrounds, including, but not limited to, computer science, political science and public policy, economics, geography, visual art, civic innovation and leadership, and more.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. We want a diverse array of skills present at the scopeAthon, which means that even if you can’t code, we want you there!

Is there a competition? Are there prizes?

Teams will present their work to a panel of expert judges and compete for prizes at the end of the day on Saturday. But the real prize for our civic hackathon will be the opportunity for all teams to continue their work with the help of IOP’s Student Civic Engagement Program, South Side Civic, and other campus resources beyond the hackathon.

What’s this problem-scoping thing? Do you have an example?

Here’s an example of a civic problem and successful solution:

Organization: Illinois Department of Human Services -- Better Birth Outcomes Program

Problem/objective: Optimizing locations for birth-outcomes clinics given limited resources

Limitation: Only 2,000 mothers can be served

Data: Birth outcomes data, census tract data

Improved Technique: Switching from analysis by county to analysis by census tract

Solution: Build a map by census tract that better illustrates the number and proportion of birth outcomes in a given area


Here are examples of the types of projects we won’t be working on:

Data exploration without a question/problem in mind (i.e. "Here’s all of our data, now tell us something!")

Building a website or app/making your website/app prettier

How do I join a team?

Register for a team when you sign up on Eventbrite. Check out our website beforehand and find some problems you’re interested in working on! We will keep updating the website with organizations that will be at the scopeAthon.

Do I need a laptop?

A laptop will be useful on Saturday. If you can’t bring one on Saturday, contact us, ChicagoSouthSideCivic(at)gmail.com and we will work something out.

Do I have to be there both days?

No. We will be updating our website with organizations at the scopeAthon online, you can still join a team on Saturday. If you can only make it on Friday, and sharpening your problem-scoping skills with a team of talented individuals for organizations that are making a difference doesn’t interest you, that’s fine too.

Do I have to stay the whole time on Saturday?

Saturday’s workshops build upon one another to provide a “problem-scoping toolkit” by the end of the event. Coming for just the afternoon or just the morning will diminish your opportunity to fully engage in the hackathon. So, email us if you have concerns about scheduling conflicts.

Who’s planning this and how can I get involved?

The civic scopeAthono is a joint effort with South Side Civic, a CAPP student-led group, UChicago’s Department of Computer Science, and the Harris School of Public Policy.

Contact ChicagoSouthSideCivic(at)gmail.com to learn more, or volunteer at the event!
